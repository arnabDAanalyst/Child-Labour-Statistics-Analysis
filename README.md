# Key KPIs
KPI 1: Average Child Labor Rate (%)
Definition:
Average percentage of children involved in labor across all countries.
Why it matters:
Shows the overall global severity of child labor.
Insight use:
Track whether child labor is improving or worsening over time.

KPI 2: Total Estimated Children in Labor (Millions)
Definition:
Sum of Estimated_Children_Millions.
Why it matters:
Represents the actual scale of the problem in population terms.
Insight use:
Helps policymakers prioritize funding and intervention.

KPI 3: Country with Highest Child Labor Rate
Definition:
Country with the maximum Child_Labor_Rate_Percent.
Why it matters:
Identifies high-risk countries requiring urgent attention.

KPI 4: Region-wise Average Child Labor Rate
Definition:
Average child labor rate grouped by Region.
Why it matters:
Highlights which regions are most affected.

KPI 5: Gender-wise Child Labor Participation
Definition:
Comparison of child labor rate or estimated children by Gender.
Why it matters:
Reveals whether boys or girls are more affected.
